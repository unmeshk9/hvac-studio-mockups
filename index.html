07 ‚Äî Objection Handling

Common Objections & Responses

Pricing Concern
"RevenueCat's percentage of MTR seems expensive at scale."

I understand the concern. Let's look at the total cost picture: Our customers typically save 30-45% of backend engineering time that would otherwise go to maintaining subscription infrastructure. At ‚Ç¨160K/year for an engineer in London or Berlin, that's significant.

Plus, with our Web Billing and Paddle partnership, you can reduce App Store fees by up to 27% on US iOS users by routing through web checkout. RC Capital also provides next-day payouts for 80% of your app store revenue ‚Äî improving cash flow that can fund growth.

Would it help to build an ROI model specific to your situation?

Build vs. Buy
"We've already built our own subscription system."

That's actually our most common starting point with larger publishers. The question isn't whether your current system works ‚Äî I'm sure it does for basic functionality.

The question is: what's the opportunity cost? Can your system run paywall A/B tests without app updates? Do you have real-time LTV analytics across iOS, Android, and web? Can you offer promotional pricing experiments next week?

Companies like [reference similar EMEA customer] found that migrating freed their team to focus on product differentiation rather than billing infrastructure. What would your engineering team build if they weren't maintaining StoreKit updates?

Data Privacy / GDPR
"We have strict data privacy requirements. How does RevenueCat handle GDPR?"

Great question ‚Äî it's one we hear often from EMEA customers. RevenueCat is SOC 2 Type II certified and fully GDPR compliant. We act as a data processor, not controller, meaning you retain control of your customer data.

We offer data processing agreements (DPAs), support data deletion requests, and can configure data retention policies to match your requirements.

Would your security team like to review our compliance documentation? I can also connect you with our Trust & Security team for a deeper dive.

Lock-in Concern
"We're worried about vendor lock-in with a critical system."

Vendor lock-in is a legitimate concern for critical infrastructure. Here's how we address it:

First, your app always maintains direct relationships with Apple and Google ‚Äî RevenueCat sits alongside, not between, you and the app stores. Your customers pay through the stores, and your developer accounts remain yours.

Second, you own your data. You can export everything, set up webhooks to your own systems, and we have documented migration paths if you ever decide to move.

Third, consider the alternative lock-in: building internally locks you into maintaining that codebase forever. With RevenueCat, you're locked into getting better features automatically ‚Äî we shipped Paywalls V2, Web Billing, and RC Capital all in the last year.

Timing
"This isn't a priority right now."

I appreciate the honesty. Can I ask what is the current priority? Often subscription infrastructure becomes urgent at specific moments ‚Äî a new app launch, cross-platform expansion, or when the team realizes they can't run experiments fast enough.

If any of those are on your roadmap for the next 6-12 months, it's worth exploring now. Integrating RevenueCat is much easier before launching a new product than migrating existing subscribers later.

Would it make sense to stay in touch? I can share relevant case studies or benchmark data from your vertical in the meantime.

Competitor Comparison
"We're also looking at [Qonversion/Purchasely]."

They're solid products ‚Äî we respect them as competitors. Here's how I'd frame the decision:

RevenueCat powers 1 in 3 new subscription apps globally and processes $10B+ annually. That scale means battle-tested reliability (ChatGPT, Notion, VSCO all depend on us), comprehensive benchmarks for your vertical, and a product roadmap driven by the largest ecosystem data in the industry.

We also have Web Billing with Paddle, RC Capital for daily payouts, Paywalls V2 with AI generation, and Funnels for web-to-app conversion ‚Äî capabilities that took years to build but you get immediately.

What matters most to you in this decision? I want to make sure we're addressing your actual requirements, not just listing features.
